## 🎯 Supply Chain Roles I'm Built For

### 📦 Supply Chain Analyst
> *Turning raw data into supply chain intelligence*

I approach supply chain analysis the way an engineer approaches a broken machine — systematically, with a root-cause mindset. My work spans:

- **Demand Forecasting Models** — built using historical trend analysis, seasonality decomposition, and moving average techniques in Excel and Power BI
- **Inventory Health Dashboards** — tracking days-on-hand, reorder points, ABC classification, and carrying cost ratios
- **Supplier Performance Scorecards** — evaluating vendors across lead time adherence, defect rates, cost variance, and responsiveness
- **Cost-to-Serve Analysis** — breaking down total landed cost across procurement, warehousing, and last-mile delivery

What sets me apart: I don't produce reports — I produce **decision frameworks**. Every analysis I do is built to answer "what should we do next?"

---

### 🏭 Operations Executive (Manufacturing & Production)
> *Where systems thinking meets shop floor reality*

My production engineering background gives me an uncommon lens for operations — I understand the physical constraints that most analysts overlook:

- **Line Balancing & Throughput Optimization** — analyzing cycle time, bottleneck identification, and WIP reduction using lean principles
- **Process Mapping & Value Stream Analysis** — visualizing material and information flows to eliminate non-value-added steps
- **OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)** — tracking availability, performance, and quality to maximize asset utilization
- **Cost Reduction Engineering** — identifying design and process changes that reduce material waste, rework, and downtime

I've designed and fabricated real production machinery. I know what optimization looks like not just on a spreadsheet — but in steel, tolerances, and cycle time.

---

### 🚛 Transportation Planning
> *Engineering the last mile and everything before it*

Logistics is where supply chain strategy lives or dies. My focus in transportation planning:

- **Network Design Analysis** — evaluating hub-and-spoke vs. point-to-point models based on volume, cost, and service constraints
- **Transportation Cost Modelling** — building cost-per-unit-mile analyses factoring in load factors, mode shifts, and consolidation opportunities
- **Route Optimization Frameworks** — applying distance-based heuristics and time-window constraints to reduce fleet cost
- **Carrier Performance Tracking** — monitoring on-time delivery, damage rates, freight cost variance, and lane performance
- **Mode Selection Strategy** — analysing trade-offs between road, rail, and air freight for different product categories

## 🧮 Engineering Philosophy

```python
class SupplyChainEngineer:

        self.name         = "Athul T Suresh"
        self.perspective  = "Systems Thinking"
```
